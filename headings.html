Headings present two problems: structural and typographic. Cure the structural problem and the typographic problem becomes simpler.

The structural problem is that lawyers often use too many levels of headings. This leads to increasingly desperate attempts to make them visually distinct, usually with injudicious combinations of bold or italic, underlining, point size, all caps, and first-line indents.
